What We Do

We Currently Run the Following Projects

Each initiative is designed to create lasting impact at the intersection of environmental sustainability and community wellbeing.

1 Climate Education
Project One

Ngite Climate Auditorium for Coffee & Cocoa Stakeholders

This project is designed to disseminate climate ideas that are worth spreading. We organize public plays, performances and presentations that are uniquely designed to convey an environmental action agenda.

The performances are open to the stakeholders in the coffee and cocoa value chains who become the audience to learn and unlearn the climatic misinformation in the form of performing arts and plays. A collection of professional climate activists from different corners of the world form the presenters' base.

Venue is currently mobile — check for the next Climate Auditorium event!

2 Food Security
Project Two

Food Security for Coffee & Cocoa Growing Communities

Climate change injustices have caused food insecurity and the associated issues of malnutrition in highly vulnerable communities. Ngite Space supports coffee and cocoa producing communities that are hit hardest by climate change catastrophes — floods, landslides, hailstones, high heat waves — to attain adaptive capacity in terms of quality food access and affordability.

We support through community education on sustainable agricultural technologies like irrigation, and through giving physical food items to the most affected in the worst scenarios. We identify climate resilient food crop seeds that are fast maturing and supply them to households for planting.

Currently working with 100 households in Kasese district, most being victims of River Nyamwamba floods

3 Gender & Climate
Project Three

Women for Climate

Putting women and girls under the cocoa and coffee value chains in the drive for environmental protection has potential to change the narrative. Women are the mothers and their ethics easily get passed onto the next generations.

Ngite Space has designed a Girls' methodology to ensure their active involvement in environmental restoration and protection movements. This approach empowers women as leaders of climate resilience in their communities.

Empowering women and girls as climate action leaders in coffee & cocoa communities

4 Waste Management
Project Four

Responsible Waste Disposal

We collect domestic and industrial waste, sort it and help to dispose it in a more responsible way. The way waste is treated prior to disposal greatly determines the amount of Greenhouse Gas emissions released. This project is aimed at reducing Greenhouse Gases resulting from poor handling of waste.

Ngite Space has established a waste treatment site where waste is sorted and treated before disposal. The plastic waste is removed and changed for recycling. Under this project we aim at making use of the cradle-to-cradle principle, making waste a raw material for new products through cleaner production innovations.

More than 5,000 MT of waste released daily — we enable responsible, sustainable disposal
